Groundnut Cultivation Guide

Introduction

Groundnut, also known as peanut, is an important oilseed crop grown in many parts of India. It is widely used for producing edible oil, peanut butter, snacks, and animal feed. Groundnut is rich in protein, healthy fats, and nutrients. Proper farming practices such as soil preparation, irrigation management, fertilization, and pest control help farmers achieve better yield and quality pods.

Suitable Climate

Groundnut grows best in warm climates with temperatures between 20°C and 30°C. It requires moderate rainfall and plenty of sunlight for healthy crop growth. Very cold or extremely wet conditions may reduce crop productivity.

Land Preparation

Proper land preparation is important for groundnut farming. The field should be ploughed deeply to loosen the soil and remove weeds. The soil should be well leveled to ensure proper drainage and uniform irrigation. Adding organic manure during land preparation improves soil fertility.

Soil Requirement

Groundnut grows well in sandy loam or well-drained light soils. The soil should be loose and friable so the pods can develop properly under the soil surface. The ideal soil pH ranges from 6.0 to 7.5.

Seed Selection

Farmers should select healthy, disease-free seeds from certified sources. High-yielding and disease-resistant varieties help improve crop production and quality.

Seed Sowing

Groundnut seeds are sown directly into the field. Proper spacing between rows and plants allows better root growth and pod development. Seeds should be sown at the correct depth to ensure uniform germination.

Irrigation

Groundnut requires moderate irrigation. Water is especially important during flowering and pod formation stages. Over-irrigation should be avoided because waterlogging can damage the roots and reduce crop yield.

Fertilizer Management

Groundnut crops require balanced nutrients for proper growth. Farmers usually apply nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers based on soil testing. Organic manure and compost also help improve soil fertility and crop productivity.

Weed Management

Weeds compete with groundnut plants for nutrients, water, and sunlight. Regular weeding helps maintain healthy crop growth. Farmers may remove weeds manually or use recommended herbicides.

Pest Management

Common pests affecting groundnut include aphids, leaf miners, and termites. Farmers should regularly inspect the field to detect pests early. Integrated pest management practices and eco-friendly pesticides help control these pests effectively.

Disease Management

Groundnut crops can be affected by diseases such as leaf spot, rust, and root rot. Using resistant varieties and applying recommended fungicides helps reduce crop damage.

Harvesting

Groundnut is ready for harvesting when the leaves turn yellow and the pods inside the soil are fully developed. Farmers uproot the plants carefully and allow them to dry in the field before removing the pods.

Post-Harvest Handling

After harvesting, groundnut pods are separated from the plants and dried properly to reduce moisture content. Proper drying improves storage life and prevents fungal infections.

Importance of Groundnut Farming

Groundnut farming plays an important role in oilseed production and rural economy. It provides income to farmers and raw materials for food and oil industries. With modern farming techniques and efficient irrigation systems, farmers can increase groundnut productivity.
