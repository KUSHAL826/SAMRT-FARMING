Smart Crop Prediction System

Our platform helps farmers identify the best crops to cultivate by analyzing farm conditions, weather patterns, soil type, and market demand.

How Our Crop Prediction Works

The Smart Demand-Based Crop Planning Platform helps farmers choose the most suitable crops for their land using modern technology and data analysis.

Farmers often face challenges when deciding which crop to grow. Different crops require different soil types, weather conditions, and water availability. Choosing the wrong crop can lead to low yield and financial loss.

To solve this problem, our platform provides an intelligent crop prediction system that analyzes multiple factors and provides accurate recommendations.

Step 1: Enter Farm Details

The first step is for the farmer to provide information about their land. Farmers enter details such as:

  • Land Area
  • Soil Type
  • Water Availability
  • District and Village Location

Step 2: Environmental Data Analysis

After the farm details are submitted, the system automatically collects environmental information such as temperature, humidity, rainfall, and weather forecasts using weather APIs. This information helps the system understand the climatic conditions of the region.

Step 3: Market Demand Analysis

The platform also analyzes current market demand data for different crops. The administrator uploads demand information such as crop name, demand quantity, and expected market price. This helps farmers grow crops that are in high demand in the market.

Step 4: Crop Prediction

Using all collected data including soil type, weather conditions, water availability, and market demand, the system processes the information through the crop prediction engine.

Step 5: Recommendation Results

The system then recommends crops in three categories:

  • Grow – High demand and suitable farming conditions
  • Caution – Moderate demand or moderate risk
  • Avoid – Low demand or unsuitable farming conditions

These recommendations help farmers make informed decisions about which crops to cultivate, improving productivity and reducing agricultural risks.
