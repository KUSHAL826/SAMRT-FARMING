# GNA Smart Farming

## Project Overview

GNA Smart Farming is a web-based platform designed to provide farmers with clear and structured crop cultivation strategies. The aim of this project is to make agricultural knowledge easily accessible so farmers can improve productivity and make better farming decisions.

This project was developed by Kushal YN Gowda, a student who is interested in using technology to solve real-world problems in agriculture.

## Problem Statement

Farmers face several challenges in modern agriculture such as:

- Lack of structured information about crop cultivation
- Crop losses due to pests and diseases
- Improper irrigation and fertilizer usage
- Unpredictable weather conditions
- Price imbalance between farmers and consumers
- Dependence on middlemen in agricultural markets

Due to these issues, farmers often struggle to maintain stable income and sustainable farming practices.

---

## Proposed Solution

GNA Smart Farming provides a simple digital platform where users can learn about crop cultivation strategies.

The website includes information about:

- Soil requirements
- Irrigation methods
- Fertilizer management
- Pest control techniques
- Harvesting methods

This structured information helps farmers and learners understand how to grow crops more efficiently.

---

## Balancing Farmer and Consumer Prices

One of the major goals of this project is to highlight the importance of maintaining a balance between farmer income and consumer prices.

In many cases, farmers sell crops at very low prices while consumers purchase the same crops at higher prices in markets. This happens due to supply chain inefficiencies and lack of market awareness.

By improving crop productivity and reducing crop losses through better farming practices, farmers can produce higher yields and maintain stable market supply. This can help farmers earn fair income while consumers can access food at reasonable prices.
